Introduction

This 911 Information and Emergency Calling Policy ("Policy") outlines the terms and conditions under which More4Less Plans ("we", "us", or "our") provides 911 emergency calling services to our customers. This Policy is designed to comply with the regulations set forth by the Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC) and to inform you about the limitations and responsibilities associated with using our phone services for emergency calling.

1. 911 Emergency Calling

1.1. Traditional 911 vs. VoIP 911

If you are using a traditional landline phone service provided by us, your 911 calls will be routed to the appropriate Public Safety Answering Point (PSAP). If you are using a VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) service, your 911 calls may be routed differently, and there may be limitations compared to traditional 911 service.

1.2. Location Information

When you place a 911 call using our services, you must provide accurate location information. Failure to provide the correct address and location information may result in delays or the inability of emergency responders to locate you.

2. Limitations of Liability

2.1. Service Interruptions

911 services provided through our phone plans may not function during service outages or when your internet connection is down. We are not liable for any failure or delay in providing 911 service due to power outages, network failures, or other technical issues beyond our control.

2.2. Non-Traditional 911 Limitations

VoIP services rely on your internet connection and electrical power. In the event of an outage, you may not be able to access 911 services. It is your responsibility to maintain an alternate means of contacting emergency services, such as a traditional landline or a mobile phone.

2.3. Accuracy of Information

We are not responsible for any inaccuracies in the information you provide to us, including your address and location details. It is your responsibility to ensure that your account information is accurate and up-to-date.

3. Customer Responsibilities

3.1. Update Location Information

You must promptly update your location information with us if you move or change your address. This ensures that your 911 calls are routed to the appropriate emergency response center.

3.2. Alternative Access

We strongly recommend that you maintain an alternative means of accessing 911 services, such as a mobile phone, in case of service interruptions or technical issues with our services.

4. Compliance with CRTC Regulations

4.1. Regulatory Compliance
